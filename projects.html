Research Projects

Selected projects and research directions, organized by theme.

Quantum and Analog Computing

My work in quantum computation focuses on the design and modeling of hybrid analog–digital systems, quantum annealers, and superconducting architectures. Recent efforts include developing protocols for energy-efficient computation, and modeling how disorder, noise, and collective dynamics affect quantum hardware.

  • Overcoming Disorder in Superconducting Globally-Driven Quantum Computing (arXiv:2510.25996)
  • Globally-Controlled Quantum Processors with ZZ Interactions (arXiv:2509.09271)
  • Classical Criticality via Quantum Annealing (Nature Communications, 2025)

Quantum Thermodynamics and Quantum Batteries

I explore the thermodynamics of quantum systems — in particular, the statistical and energetic limits of computation, storage, and thermalization at the quantum scale. This includes foundational work on the theory of quantum batteries, quantum thermodynamic uncertainty relations, and the role of coherence in energy transport.

  • Universally-Charging Protocols for Quantum Batteries: A No-Go Theorem (Eur. Phys. Lett., 2025)
  • Energy Storage and Coherence in Closed and Open Quantum Batteries (Quantum, 2021)
  • Operator-Based Quantum Thermodynamic Uncertainty Relations (arXiv:2406.11974)
  • Random Quantum Batteries (Phys. Rev. Research, 2020)

Neuromorphic and Memristive Computing

I study physical computation through networks of memristive devices and nanomagnets, developing theories of learning and optimization in physical substrates. My group investigates how these systems can be used for analog computing, optimization, and machine learning in hardware.

  • Uncontrolled Learning: Co-design of Neuromorphic Hardware Topology (Adv. Intelligent Systems, 2025)
  • Voltage-Controlled Oscillator and Memristor-Based Analog Computing (arXiv:2506.09392)
  • Self-Organizing Memristive Networks as Physical Learning Systems (Nature Reviews in Physics, 2025)

Complex Systems and Network Theory

I apply tools from statistical physics and graph theory to the study of self-organizing systems, ecological and economic networks, and the dynamics of computation. This includes work on centrality measures, conservation laws, and network thermodynamics.

  • Distribution of Centrality Measures on Undirected Random Networks (PNAS, 2024)
  • A Unifying Approach to Self-Organizing Systems Interacting via Conservation Laws (2025)
  • Correlation between Upstreamness and Downstreamness in Global Value Chains (JEBO, 2025)

Fundamental and Theoretical Physics

My background in theoretical and mathematical physics spans from quantum gravity and statistical mechanics to the study of emergent space and computation in physical systems. I continue to explore connections between physics, complexity, and information theory.

  • Disordered Locality and Lorentz Dispersion Relations: An Explicit Model of Quantum Foam (Phys. Rev. D, 2012)
  • Quantum Bose-Hubbard Model with Evolving Graph as Toy Model for Emergent Spacetime (Phys. Rev. D, 2010)
  • PhD Thesis: Quantum Pre-Geometric Models for Quantum Gravity (University of Waterloo, 2012)

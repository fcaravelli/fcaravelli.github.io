  • Artificial Spin Ice Simulator

  • Evolving Networks with Memory
    MATLAB package for simulating networks with reinforcing walkers, developed from the research published in EPL 109, 28006 (2015). This software models evolving and growing networks subject to reinforcement dynamics and decay, with support for multiple types of dynamics, directed or undirected graphs, weighted links, and control over initial graph structure. The simulator can analyze clustering, thermalization times, and graph observables, and can also generate animations such as this example video.

NonLinTB: Nonlinear Time Series State Space Reconstruction

Repository: github.com/fcaravelli/NonLinTB

NonLinTB is a MATLAB toolbox for the graphical reconstruction of state spaces from nonlinear time series data. It allows users to explore the underlying dynamics of complex systems by embedding time series into higher-dimensional manifolds, visualizing attractors, and analyzing their structure.

The toolbox integrates and extends several existing algorithms developed by other researchers, combining them with original routines for visualization and nonlinear analysis. It is designed for researchers interested in chaos, complex systems, and nonlinear dynamics.

  • Written in MATLAB with modular functions
  • Supports delay embedding and manifold visualization
  • Compatible with existing nonlinear time series toolboxes
  • Includes example datasets and scripts for quick testing

About Me

Last updated: October 2025

Origins

Born in Palmi, Calabria, on June 6th, 1985 — in that mythical, often forgotten corner of Southern Europe. According to some I was Italian; for others, simply a terrone. Historians agree only that I was the outcome of an unlikely symmetry breaking between a communist and a fascist — an event that, according to physicists, occurred within a Planck-sized sphere over a Planck-time duration.

I speak italiano, English, Deutsch, and Calabbrisi. I consider myself a European of Italian nationality — or, more precisely, Calabrian.

Facts (Not Too Seriously)

I was a Staff Scientist at Los Alamos National Laboratory between 2020 and 2025. Being from Southern Italy, it’s statistically unlikely that I’ll become famous — but that’s fine.

I have few hobbies. I find squirrels and chipmunks funny, and I’m not particularly fond of spiders. I sometimes dream of living on Craven Road no. 8, next door to Dylan Dog, my hero (or anti-hero).

My favorite athlete used to be Oscar Pistorius. Now I root for Alex Zanardi. To me, sport is about struggle rather than success.

I also sell zero-point energy — Nullpunktenergie if you prefer the German sound — for the fair price of £25 per box. Zero-Point Energy: Zero-Point in buying it!

Things I Like

Flickr used to be awesome. GNU/Linux Ubuntu was even cooler.

I used to read Repubblica, Corriere, Linkiesta, now I only read Il Post; they often disagree with each other and with themselves, which is comforting.

According to a random internet quiz, I am a night owl.

Ashes of Rome

Remo did not know that his brother
would kill him for the power of Rome.
Caesar felt the cold blade of Brutus
and cried for forgiveness.
Caligula should have known
that Claudius would sell his soul to the devil himself
so that Virginia and Nero could burn Rome to ashes.
This is why I canceled my flight to Rome.
Damnatio Memoriae.

Where I’ve Lived

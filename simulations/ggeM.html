Hard Disks in a Central Potential — tunable mass M, no walls

Central attraction ∝ M / r², no hard walls. With large M and tangential annulus init you get bound, ring-like orbits; with small M and high energy some disks fly off (“escape to infinity” within this finite window). Equal masses, hard-disk collisions.
